Apollo Guidance Computer

The Apollo Guidance Computer (AGC) holds a significant place in history as the pioneering computer system that facilitated humanity's journey to the Moon. Despite its monumental achievement, the AGC bears certain limitations compared to modern technology. It is bulky, relatively slow, and notably deficient in both memory and storage capacity. While groundbreaking for its era, advancements in technology have rendered it comparatively outdated.

Surprisingly, the AGC's capabilities can now be eclipsed by a humble $4 microcontroller. These modern microcontrollers, while incredibly affordable, boast impressive performance metrics that surpass the AGC's processing power. This stark juxtaposition highlights the rapid progress of technology over time, showcasing how even the most revolutionary innovations can be surpassed by simpler, more cost-effective solutions in the ever-evolving landscape of computing.
